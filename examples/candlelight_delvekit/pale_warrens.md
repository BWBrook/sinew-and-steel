# The Floor That Chooses

A drowned ferry route turned kill-box, fought over by two local crews who both want the same cache and neither wants to be first through the worst room.

## Premise

The Pale Warrens lie below the under-river cuts, where an old ferry route was swallowed by black water and never stopped being useful to desperate people. Varro Dane, an eel trapper, wants whatever lies past the site's worst chamber badly enough to pay in safe passage through the next customs watch. The problem is plain: one chamber in the middle of the route was built to kill the hasty, a local brute has learned to own that same ground, and two rival scavenger bands are already circling the old customs cache.

This is a small hard delve. The site is short, but it is not forgiving.

## Progression at a Glance

- `1. Drip Mouth` is the hub. From there the delvers can push south into one faction's pocket, north toward a puzzle room and useful salvage, or east toward the real hazard.
- `3. Underpool Walk` is the kill-room and the heart of the site. It is also where the solo threat and one faction overlap, so this room should feel like the route everyone wants and no one wants to take.
- `4. Tide Wheel` is the safest leverage point in the dungeon: a puzzle room, a treasure room, and the site of the only weird survivor.
- `7. Maw Hollow` is the deep chamber and boss room, reached only by crossing the worst ground in the site.
- The factions do not want abstract evil. They want the same old customs cache and the route that reaches it.

## Room Key

### 1. Drip Mouth
- **Description:** The entry ledge into the warrens, scarred by dragged cargo and just wide enough to make returning with loot sound more plausible than it is.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 4, south passage -> 2, east passage -> 3
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** a narrow ledge scarred by dragged cargo
- **Atmosphere:** a low roar from water moving where you cannot see

### 2. Drip Chamber
- **Description:** A claimed side chamber held by the Mooring Order, who have turned it into a watch-point over one branch of the route.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 1
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** Mooring Order sentries with short bows, tarred knives, and climbing claws
- **Atmosphere:** a faint salt breeze from the wrong direction

They want the old customs cache and have no intention of letting rivals pass unbloodied.

### 3. Underpool Walk
- **Description:** The site’s true problem: a crossing chamber where the flood marks are wrong, the warnings are honest, and the room is built to make every rescue slower than the last.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 1, south passage -> 7, east passage -> 5
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** warning plaques, old bones in a bad place, Mire Threshers sentries with harpoons, split shields, and cork-floats, signs of silt leeches, a Warm-Pool Hydra
- **Atmosphere:** mildewed rope and old pitch

This is the Grimtooth-style lethal room. Telegraph it clearly. The danger should feel unfair only to the delver who ignores the warnings.

### 4. Tide Wheel
- **Description:** A water-work chamber built around a real mechanism, not scenery. The wheel still matters to movement through the site.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 6, south passage -> 1
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** tide wheel that accepts specific rope tensions, a rule tied to cave hydraulics, netted trade seal, barefoot night watchman with blood on one sleeve and none of it theirs
- **Atmosphere:** a low roar from water moving where you cannot see

This is the site’s puzzle room. It should feel like old ferry infrastructure that can still be understood by careful players.

### 5. Broken Ferry
- **Description:** A one-way-feeling side room and faction hold where the Mire Threshers have been watching the kill-room and waiting for someone else to pay first.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 3
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** clear traces of Mire Threshers passage
- **Atmosphere:** a low roar from water moving where you cannot see

### 6. Salt Pocket
- **Description:** A cramped northern pocket held by the Mooring Order, useful mostly as a fallback position and proof that the faction spread here is practical rather than theatrical.
- **Visible exits:** south passage -> 4
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** clear traces of Mooring Order passage
- **Atmosphere:** air that smells of eel skin and wet leather

### 7. Maw Hollow
- **Description:** The deep chamber at the end of the bad route, where the site’s ruling threat still holds the last argument.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 3
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** The Warder of the Drowned Shelves holds court here
- **Atmosphere:** mildewed rope and old pitch

This is the boss room. Reaching it should feel earned because the party had to choose the kill-room knowingly.

## Factions and Threats

- **Mooring Order** are cave fishers with rules older than the town above. They want the old customs cache and prefer to feign retreat toward a crossing they already trust or have already trapped.
- **Mire Threshers** want the same cache and favor aggression against whoever carries the best light. They are not grand villains. They are local, armed, and desperate.
- **Warm-Pool Hydra** makes the central route worse than a trap alone. It changes how the party treats noise, light, and wounded retreat.
- **The Warder of the Drowned Shelves** is the final holdout in the deep chamber.

## Treasure

- `4. Tide Wheel`: netted trade seal
- whatever lies beyond the kill-room for the delvers bold enough to claim it

This site works because the treasure is not abstract. The promise of payoff sits visibly close to the danger and farther beyond it.

## Running It

Run this as a short, mean cavern job where the players can see the bad decision and choose it anyway. The factions are there to apply pressure and make hesitation costly, not to drown the session in negotiation procedure. On hard, `Underpool Walk` should feel survivable only if the players read the room, respect the warnings, and treat retreat as part of play rather than failure.
