# The Last Rite of Dusty Scripture

A medium reliquary delve of locked inner rooms, rival scavenger sects, old shrine logic, and a rite that is already too far along.

## Premise

Vaulted House lies below the chapel roads, in a candle vault where shrine silver vanished a century ago and bad devotion lingered after the theft. Rhea Yarrow, a lamp porter, is paying in hard coin for anyone who can stop whatever is building in the lower shrine before it breaks fully into the upper ways. The site still has structure: a central hall, a sealed western route, faction-held rooms, two real puzzle chambers, and one deep sanctum where the work below has a master.

This is a medium dangerous delve. It should feel survivable to careful players and punishing to proud ones.

## Progression at a Glance

- `1. Pilgrim Descent` opens into a three-way start: north to early treasure and faction signs, south to the hidden-route side of the site, and east into the central hall.
- `2. Forked Hall` is the main hub. The obvious west-to-deep route is not here; the real western push is blocked farther on.
- `3. Collapsed Scriptorium` shows the first real barrier: the `spoken seal` to `5. Upper Grate`.
- The `Saint Phrase` needed for that seal lies in `8. Abbot's Chest`, reachable from the lower side and easier to find if the players discover the secret link from `4. Choir Stalls`.
- `5. Upper Grate` is the approach room for the solo threat and the throat of the deep wing.
- `9. The Last Shrine` is both the boss chamber and one of the site’s puzzle rooms.
- `10. Mask Chapel` is the other puzzle room and helps make the deep wing feel like chapel-work, not random dungeon furniture.

## Room Key

### 1. Pilgrim Descent
- **Description:** The old entry into the vault: a processional descent marked by turned saint markers that already suggest the shrine’s route logic was once reserved knowledge.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 7, south passage -> 4, east passage -> 2
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** saint markers turned inward as if they hide a route meant for clergy eyes only
- **Atmosphere:** old brass gone cold in the dark

### 2. Forked Hall
- **Description:** The central decision room, broad enough to split the party’s intentions even before the dungeon tries.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 1, north passage -> 3, south passage -> 8, east passage -> 6
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** offering bowls green with age
- **Atmosphere:** funeral flowers gone sweet with rot

### 3. Collapsed Scriptorium
- **Description:** A held room where scripture, ash, and old passage records have become barricade and warning both.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 7, south passage -> 2, east passage -> 5
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** Ratling Book sentries with relic cases worn as chest armor, signs of ash crows niche dwellers
- **Atmosphere:** bells trembling somewhere out of sight

The east passage is blocked by a `spoken seal`. It opens if the party carries the `Saint Phrase`.

### 4. Choir Stalls
- **Description:** A lower side chamber claimed by the Bier Fold, useful mostly because it hides the site’s one secret bypass.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 1
- **Hidden exits:** east passage -> 8
- **Contents:** Bier Fold sentries with processional poles, crowbars, and chipped mail; signs of relic leeches niche dwellers
- **Atmosphere:** dusty prayer air and lamp smoke

This is where careful players can find the shortcut to the key room.

### 5. Upper Grate
- **Description:** The deep threshold of the reliquary, where the halls narrow and the hunter begins to own the route.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 3, north passage -> 9, south passage -> 6, east passage -> 10
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** clear traces of Bier Fold passage, the Ash-Marked Lector
- **Atmosphere:** cold stone rubbed smooth by processions

This room changes movement. Once the players enter it, the site should stop feeling merely mapped and start feeling hunted.

### 6. Furnace Landing
- **Description:** A plain transitional chamber of shrine debris and old heat, useful mainly because it makes the surrounding rooms feel worked and connected.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 2, north passage -> 5
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** shrine fragments and cracked icons
- **Atmosphere:** cold stone rubbed smooth by processions

### 7. Silver Locker
- **Description:** An early payoff room under Bier Fold pressure, good enough to tempt greed before the site has earned trust.
- **Visible exits:** south passage -> 1, east passage -> 3
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** gospel-bound votive coffer, traces of Bier Fold passage
- **Atmosphere:** bells trembling somewhere out of sight

### 8. Abbot's Chest
- **Description:** The key room: part prize cache, part doctrinal dead end, and exactly the sort of place players should want to revisit once they know what they have found.
- **Visible exits:** north passage -> 2
- **Hidden exits:** west passage -> 4
- **Contents:** Saint Phrase, ember-cut votive plate, traces of Ratling Book passage
- **Atmosphere:** wet limestone and candle ash

This room holds the `Saint Phrase`, which opens the spoken seal at `3 -> 5`.

### 9. The Last Shrine
- **Description:** The deep sanctum where chapel logic and final threat meet. The room’s mechanism is part of the ritual authority here, not decoration.
- **Visible exits:** south passage -> 5
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** saint statue array tied to processional logic, traces of Ratling Book passage, The Abbot Beneath the Lower Nave
- **Atmosphere:** a hush broken only by chain and drip

This is the boss room and one of the site’s major puzzles.

### 10. Mask Chapel
- **Description:** A working side chapel whose mechanism teaches players how much of the shrine was process, not mystery.
- **Visible exits:** west passage -> 5
- **Hidden exits:** none
- **Contents:** funeral bier scale that accepts relic fragments by rank
- **Atmosphere:** wet limestone and candle ash

This room should feel like integrated shrine-work, not a detached puzzle minigame.

## Factions and Threats

- **Bier Fold** are starved candle-keepers turned territorial. They want the shrine stores beneath the lower nave and favor curtains, smoke, and side doors to break line of sight.
- **Ratling Book** are grave opportunists hiding behind thin piety. They want to rekindle an old saint-fire and use barred doors and lamp smoke to split intruders.
- **Ash-Marked Lector** is the site’s feared solo threat. It makes the Upper Grate and deep wing behave differently from the rest of the delve.
- **The Abbot Beneath the Lower Nave** is the final authority in the shrine and the real reason delay matters.

## Treasure

- `7. Silver Locker`: gospel-bound votive coffer
- `8. Abbot's Chest`: ember-cut votive plate
- `8. Abbot's Chest`: the `Saint Phrase`, which is both key and prize

The treasure here is good old reliquary bait: portable, valuable, and placed where greed can complicate judgment.

## Running It

Run this as a medium torchlit shrine crawl with one strong backtrack: discover the seal, find the phrase, then push the deep wing under growing pressure. The factions should feel hungry and practical, not mythic. The dangers here are not just monsters; they are smoke, divided loyalties, bad route choices, and the temptation to treat shrine-work like scenery instead of instruction.
