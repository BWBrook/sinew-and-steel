# Candlelight Delvekit Quick Reference

Use this only when the dungeon itself is the challenge.

## Exploration Turn

One turn is one meaningful dungeon beat:

- move
- inspect
- search
- force
- listen
- map
- rest briefly
- parley

At the end of a risky or time-eating turn, the Custodian may:

- mark Fatigue
- advance local resource pressure
- trigger a faction reaction
- move a solo monster sign or threat
- update revealed map state

## Reveal Rules

Add a room to the player map when the delvers:

- enter it
- clearly observe it
- confirm the connection exists

Do not reveal:

- secret doors
- concealed routes
- one-way truths not yet understood

## Difficulty Dials

## Zero-Stamina Chances

- `soft`: first two hits to `0 STM` may survive with a hard cost; the next time is death
- `medium`: first hit to `0 STM` may survive with a hard cost; the next time is death
- `hard`: `0 STM` means death unless an ally stops the blow immediately

Hard costs should change the delve: forced retreat, lost gear, separation, maiming, or capture on softer modes.

### Soft

- close to stock Candlelight Dungeons
- clearer clues
- lower lethality
- slower faction pressure
- most bad calls cost position, gear, or Stamina before they kill
- two hard survivals at `0 STM`
- `heroic (16)` suggested

### Medium

- proper dangerous delve
- meaningful attrition
- active factions
- serious traps
- one bad decision is survivable; repeated bad play is not
- one hard survival at `0 STM`
- `standard (6)` suggested

### Hard

- real death risk
- harsher traps and pressure
- more ruthless faction behavior
- wasted turns should hurt
- `0 STM` means death on hard
- a couple of bad decisions can end a character
- `grim (0)` suggested

## Good Dungeon Ingredients

Not every generated or hand-built site needs all of these.

- one early blocker
- one reason to backtrack
- 0-2 puzzles
- 0-1 lethal trap room
- 0-1 solo monster
- 0-1 boss
- 0-2 factions
- at least one shortcut, loop, or secret

## Trap-Room Rule of Thumb

Most traps should maim, split, drain, or alarm.
One room per delve may be a true Grimtooth-style killer.

Telegraph it.
Let caution matter.
If badly sprung, let it kill.

## Faction Rule of Thumb

For each faction, know:

- what it wants
- what it holds
- what it fears
- what it trades
- what starts the fight

Move bodies, alarms, and routes.
Do not build a reputation mini-game.
